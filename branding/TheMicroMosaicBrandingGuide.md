## 1. Brand Voice ‚Äî Application Rules (Not Redefinition)

### Canonical Voice (from Field Guide)

MicroMosaic‚Äôs brand voice is:

* **Intelligent, grounded, and precise**
* **Warm, empathetic, and open** ‚Äî without sacrificing rigor
* **Authentic and plain‚Äëspoken** ‚Äî no posturing, no performance
* **Passionate and visibly invested** in the science and its impact
* **Collaborative and invitational** ‚Äî dialog‚Äëdriven, non‚Äëhierarchical
* **Confident and values‚Äëanchored** ‚Äî clear in expertise and boundaries, willing to compromise *only* when aligned with ethics
* **Embracing and generous** ‚Äî curious about other perspectives, generous with knowledge
* **Poetic and mythic when it serves understanding**, never when it obscures it

This voice should feel human, credible, and alive ‚Äî never corporate, sanitized, or performative.

This guide defines **where and how** to apply that voice.

---

### Microbial Respect & Framing Clause

MicroMosaic language and visuals treat microbes with respect, curiosity, and ecological awareness.

* Microbes are framed as **ecological actors and systems participants**, not enemies or contaminants
* Language avoids fear-based, moralized, or deficit-oriented framing by default
* Complexity, context, and relationship are preferred over simplification or alarm
* When pathogenicity or risk is discussed, it is done **precisely, contextually, and without sensationalism**

This applies across copy, visuals, metaphors, and educational framing.

---

### Voice by Context

**Homepage / About / Talks / Workshops**

* Light poetic framing is acceptable
* Metaphor may be used *after* clarity is established
* Emotional resonance is allowed
* Curiosity-forward language encouraged

**Case Studies / Service Pages / Proposals**

* Clear, direct, concrete
* Narrative is explanatory, not lyrical
* Focus on decisions, outcomes, and interpretation
* Metaphor only if it clarifies a process or result

**Technical Reports / Methods / Governance / Audit-Adjacent Work**

* Zero mythic framing
* Precision over tone
* Explicit assumptions, limits, and uncertainty
* Plain language > elegance

**Rule of Thumb:**
If the reader must *trust* the output, clarity always overrides style.

---

### Common Failure Modes (Avoid)

* Sounding like an academic paper on the website
* Sounding like a creative studio with no scientific backbone
* Overusing metaphor where a sentence of explanation would suffice
* Hiding uncertainty instead of naming it
* **LinkedIn‚Äëstyle professionalism**: buzzword soup, forced optimism, sanitized corporate affect, performative ‚Äúthought‚Äëleader‚Äù theater, hollow confidence, or any language that prioritizes optics over truth

**What this means in practice:**

* Avoid empty phrases like ‚Äúexcited to share,‚Äù ‚Äúthrilled to announce,‚Äù or generic leadership platitudes
* Avoid performative empathy that names feelings without offering substance
* Avoid polish that erases conviction, boundaries, or ethical stance
* Prefer directness, care, and grounded confidence over branding theater

## Collaboration Ethos (How the Work Feels)

MicroMosaic works *with* people, not over them.

Collaboration is dialog-driven, iterative, and grounded in shared sense-making. Interpretation is treated as a collective process, where questions, uncertainty, and partial understanding are welcomed rather than hidden. Expertise is exercised without dominance, and leadership shows up as clarity, care, and follow-through ‚Äî not control.

This ethos should be reflected in:

* Copy that invites conversation rather than instruction
* Case studies that highlight shared reasoning, not just outputs
* Visuals that emphasize process, flow, and connection

---

## Ethics-in-Action: When MicroMosaic Says No

Scientific integrity and values alignment take precedence over speed, optics, or convenience.

MicroMosaic will respectfully decline or redirect work that:

* Compromises scientific rigor, transparency, or reproducibility
* Requires fear-based framing, hype, or misrepresentation of results
* Conflicts with ethical standards, ecological responsibility, or honest communication

Saying no is part of the practice ‚Äî not a failure of collaboration, but a commitment to trust, integrity, and long-term impact.

## 3. Website Structure ‚Äî Field Guide ‚Üí Web Translation

### Homepage Must Answer (In Order)

1. What MicroMosaic does
2. Who it is for
3. What problem it solves
4. How it works (at a high level)
5. Proof via case studies or examples

**Homepage Copy Rules:**

* No long philosophy blocks
* No buzzwords
* No unexplained jargon

---

### Services Pages

Services should be framed as **capabilities**, not rigid packages.

Each service page should include:

* What problem this helps solve
* What collaboration looks like
* What outputs typically include
* How interpretation and communication are handled

Avoid:

* Overly long lists without context
* Tool-first framing without purpose

---

### Case Studies (Critical)

Case studies are the **primary credibility engine**.

Each case study should include:

1. Context & constraints
2. The scientific or data challenge
3. What was done (at the right technical level)
4. Interpretation & decisions enabled
5. Outcome / impact

Tone:

* Concrete
* Grounded
* Decision-oriented

## 5. Consistency Checks (Use as a Litmus Test)
